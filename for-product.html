The Problem

You designed it one way.
It shipped another.

×

Spec drift

Features launch and don't match what you specified. You discover it in production, through customer complaints, or during quarterly reviews.

×

No verification

You rely on manual testing or trust. There's no systematic way to check that what was built matches what was designed.

×

Lost in translation

The gap between your Jira ticket and the deployed code is a black box. You can't zoom from a high-level feature down to the exact screen, field, and validation rule that implements it.

How It Works

From spec to verified behavior

Terso captures your intent as structured behavior. Refine it through natural language, then verify every piece against the compiled output.

01

Capture

Describe what you want in plain language. Terso structures it into a behavior map -- from high-level user flows down to individual screen logic and field validation rules.

02

Compile

The behavior map compiles to frontend code, backend API, and automated tests. Every behavior has a traceable path from spec to implementation.

03

Verify & Iterate

Every behavior gets a status: verified, drifted, or broken. When something drifts, describe the correction and Terso updates the scheme, recompiles, and re-verifies.
